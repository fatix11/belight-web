What We Do

Our Programs

We deliver tangible and sustainable change across four priority areas that address the most urgent needs in urban and rural Ethiopia.

Program 01

Social Protection & Support

We provide holistic support to single mothers, widows, and children — including economic empowerment, skills training, psychosocial support, and essential resources — to help the most vulnerable break the cycle of poverty and secure a dignified, self-reliant future.

  • → Economic empowerment for low-income single mothers in urban areas
  • → Psychosocial support and mental health resources
  • → Essential resource distribution for children and families
  • → Community-based networks and peer support systems

Program 02

Education Facility Development

We build and renovate school facilities in rural communities to create safe, inspiring learning environments where children can thrive, dream big, and unlock their full potential — regardless of where they were born.

  • → Construction and renovation of rural school buildings
  • → Safe and inclusive learning environments for disadvantaged children
  • → Sanitation and hygiene facilities within schools
  • → Educational support and materials for underserved communities
Program 03

WASH — Water, Sanitation & Hygiene

We bring reliable access to safe drinking water to underserved rural communities — reducing disease, saving time, removing unnecessary burdens especially for women and girls, and improving the overall well-being and productivity of entire communities.

  • → Climate-resilient clean water infrastructure
  • → Sanitation facilities and hygiene promotion
  • → Community ownership and sustainability training
  • → Environmental protection and water source management

Program 04

Livelihood & Economic Empowerment

We promote sustainable livelihoods through skills development, income-generating activities, and community-driven initiatives — building household and community resilience and helping families achieve lasting financial independence.

  • → Vocational and skills training programs
  • → Micro-enterprise and income-generating activities
  • → Savings groups and financial literacy
  • → Market linkages and community-driven economic initiatives
